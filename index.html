Save the Dolphins!

Dolphins (Delphinus Delphis) are one of the smartest and most interesting animals of the sea! These aquatic mammals are scattered in every ocean of the world except the Arctic and Antarctic. These dolphin populations around the globe are facing severe threats due to human pollution of our oceans.

The chemicals and toxins from sewage runoff impair the dolphin's immunity system. Common diseases caused by these toxins are pnuemonia and skin infections. Not only do these chemicals affect the immunity system, but also their respiratory systems.Oil platforms and steam boats create pollution in the air that dolphins breathe.

As well as chemicals and toxins affecting the well being of these dolphins, physical garbage has had lasting impacts on these dolphins such as:

  • Floating aluminum cans physically cutting up the outer skin of the dolphins
  • The plastic wrapping that holds soda cans together often getting stuck on the beaks of bottlenose dolphins.
